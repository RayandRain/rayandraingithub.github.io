Research & Publications
Master's Thesis
The Dangerous "Other": Catholic Church in the PRC Official Narratives in the 1950s
Abstract: Through the lens of Imagology, this dissertation examines the Catholic Church in the PRC's official narratives in the 1950s. Using the Vatican, the Legion of Mary, and Fr. Raymond de Jaegher as examples, it integrates evidential research and text analysis of official propaganda, papal encyclicals, memoirs, digital archives, and other sources. The core argument is that the negative descriptions of the Catholic Church in the PRC official narratives were not total fabrications but constructed images through magnifying and selective usages of historical reality, to make the reform seem necessary for reintegrating the Chinese Catholic Church into the nation.

Key Contributions:
  • Applies conceptual framework of imagology to analyze Catholic Church representations
  • Examines transnational nature of Vatican's "instrument of imperialism" image
  • Analyzes contrasting descriptions of Roman Legion by different groups
  • Critically reassesses paradigms of "Cultural Invasion" and "Cultural Imperialism"
